Kim Kardashian's Sex Tape Becomes Virtual Reality 'Experience'

 Posted on September 1, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  326 words  |  Tandra Barner

This is a joke, right? Kim Kardashian‘s 2003 sex tape with her ex-boyfriend Ray J was turned into a virtual reality “experience” using a lookalike adult film star (yes, really).

Pornography studio Vivid Entertainment unveiled its latest offering, titled The Kim Kardashian Superstar Experience, which allows viewers to experience the infamous tape “using the latest immersive technology,” according to the New York Post.

The so-called “experience” begins with actual footage from Kardashian’s sex tape. Moments later, a doppelgänger steps into the frame and brings the video to life. Helmet-wearing viewers are able to interact with the lookalike as she performs sex acts on them.

In between uttering phrases — including “Hi there, I like your room” and “I know you bought the video because you wanna see me. Why don’t we just play here?” — the adult actress begins to, uh, pleasure herself with a sex toy.

Vivid Entertainment reportedly charges customers $119.97 a year for membership to the website.

Kardashian, 36, sued the adult entertainment company in 2007 after it released the original sex tape, titled Kim K Superstar. She later dropped the lawsuit and settled for a reported $5 million. It is unclear whether the new virtual reality video violates the agreement.

Eight months after the sex tape was released, the Kardashian family kicked off the first season of their E! reality series, Keeping Up With the Kardashians, which has since become an international sensation with 12 seasons and countless spinoffs.
