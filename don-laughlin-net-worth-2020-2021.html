Early Life & Biography

Donald “Don” Joseph Laughlin was born on May 4, 1931, in Owatonna, Minnesota. He is infamous for being a gambling entrepreneur and hotelier from Nevada. As a native resident of Owatonna, Don worked during the winters as a young man as a fur trapper. He then used his income to buy slot machines that he could install in the hunting lodges.

His idea got him $500 every week, and that raised certain questions among those around him. He received a warning from his principal that he must either give up school or his side business of slot machines. Don on realizing that he was anyway making thrice of what the principal earns decided to drop out.

Career

In the late ’50s, Don shifter to Las Vegas, Nevada, and bought his first casino named “101 Club”. Later in 1964, Don had sold the 101 Club. During this time, he had acquired enough wealth to be flying across the California Arizona Nevada tri-state area. After the construction of the Davis Dam in 1951, Bullhead City and Kingman in Arizona and Needles in California witnessed large masses settling in those regions.

Don used this opportunity to establish a destination resort as it was also a stop on the Route 66 freeway. He purchased an eight-room shuttered motel on 6.5 acres for $250,000. The landowner sold it to Don despite having several offers because of Don’s background in the casino business. Within two years of opening, the resort was welcoming guests who came looking for gambling opportunities as well as travelers.

The resort had twelve slot machines and two lie gambling tables. Only four rooms were available since the rest four were occupied by Don’s family. The property is now famous as the Riverside Resort and Casino. It underwent a major expansion in 1972, with 48 new rooms being added and again in 1975, where 52 new rooms were added along with a fourteen-story tower with 307 rooms and an automotive museum were added to the resort.

After it was an expansion in 1994, currently, the resort holds 1405 rooms and a bowling center being added to the resorts facilities recently. Don also played a crucial role in financing the construction for the bridge at the northern tip of Laughlin over the Colorado River and the Laughlin-Bullhead International Airport.
