Early Life and Family of Neal Bledsoe

Neal Bledsoe, a Canadian professional actor, writer, and filmmaker, was born on March 26, 1981, in the beautiful location of Toronto, Ontario, Canada.

He was born in a Caucasian ethnic group family holding the nationality of Canada.

Bledsoe grew up along with his parents in Seattle, Washington. Talking about his family, his grandfather served in the Air Force during World War II.

Similarly, his maternal grandfather was awarded the Distinguished Flying Cross for becoming part of the bombing of the Nazi oil refinery at Ploiesti.

Also, Neal is the fourth cousin of the former NFL quarterback Drew Bledsoe. However, unfortunately, the information regarding his parents is not disclosed.

Furthermore, his mother was a life insurance underwriter and estate planner. Later, she became an executive for a worldwide recognized company.

Also, his father was a Ph.D. holder in Sociology from the University of Toronto. In addition, his father studied under the philosopher Marshall McLuhan.

Neal Bledsoe: Education

Talking about his early life education, Bledsoe pursued his high school education from the Roosevelt High School in Seattle and then shifted to Garfield High School.
