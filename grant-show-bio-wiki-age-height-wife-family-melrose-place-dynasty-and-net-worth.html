Grant Show Biography

Grant Show is an American actor who was born and brought up in Detroit, Michigan, in the United States as Grant Alan Show. He is best known for his role on Melrose Place as Jake Hanson alongside Amy Locane, which he played from 1992 to 1997. Since 2017, Grant portrays tycoon Blake Carrington in the soap opera reboot Dynasty.

    Grant Show Melrose Place

    Grant broke into show business during college. His first major role was on the Ryan’s Hope daytime soap opera, where he portrayed Rick Hyde from 1984 to 1987. Grant’s appearances on television attracted the attention of producer Aaron Spelling, who felt he had star potential and decided to cast him as Jake Hanson on a few episodes of the famous series Beverly Hills, 90210 in order to spin off the character as the lead in his next series Melrose Place.

    During his time on Melrose Place, Show also appeared on an episode of Saturday Night Live hosted by Laura Leighton. In 1997, Show, Laura Leighton (an off-screen girlfriend of Show’s for a period), Doug Savant, Courtney Thorne-Smith, and Marcia Cross left Melrose Place, which led the show into a major decline. It was canceled two years later, though Show later regretted his decision to leave, despite his dislike of the scripts during his final season.

    Grant Show Dynasty

    In 2012, Grant appeared in the film The Possession, directed by Ole Bornedal, alongside Jeffrey Dean Morgan and Kyra Sedgwick. In 2013, he began his role as Spence Westmore on Devious Maids. In March 2017, he was cast in The CW’s Dynasty reboot as Blake Carrington.

    Grant Show 90210

    The Dynasty series made Grant one of the most popular and best-known actors on American television during the 1990s. He additionally played Jake in a guest appearance in the pilot of the Melrose spin-off Models Inc., making him the only actor to play the same character on the first three shows in the Beverly Hills, 90210 franchise.
