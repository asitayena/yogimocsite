What does Stoner mean in movies?

Stoner comedy is a comedic genre that revolves around characters who are regular users of marijuana (weed). These films and TV shows explore the comical situations and experiences that arise when people are under the influence of this green herb.

Known for their relaxed and often surreal humor, stoner comedies celebrate the counterculture associated with cannabis use and are known for their irreverent and unconventional style.

Origins

The origins of stoner comedy can be traced back to the countercultural movements of the 1960s and 1970s, particularly the hippie movement and the embrace of marijuana as a symbol of rebellion and non-conformity.

Early stoner comedies like Up in Smoke (1978) starring Cheech and Chong, helped establish the genre’s popularity.

Types of Stoner Comedy

Now, let’s break it down into categories:

Buddy Stoner Comedies

These films typically revolve around two or more main characters who are close friends and often partners in their marijuana-related adventures. The camaraderie and chemistry between the characters are central to the humor.

Examples:

Classic examples of buddy stoner comedies include the films starring Cheech and Chong, such as Up in Smoke. Cheech Marin and Tommy Chong play the iconic stoner duo whose escapades are fueled by their love for weed.

Surreal Stoner Comedies

Surreal stoner comedies often venture into the realm of absurdity and hallucination. They blur the line between reality and fantasy, creating a mind-bending experience for the audience.

Examples:

Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas starring Johnny Depp is a prime example of a surreal stoner comedy. The film follows the wild and drug-fueled journey of journalist Raoul Duke as he travels to Las Vegas.

High School Stoner Comedies:

This subgenre focuses on high school or college-aged characters and their misadventures while using marijuana. It often explores the challenges and absurdities of adolescence and coming of age.

Examples:

Dazed and Confused (1993) directed by Richard Linklater is a classic high school stoner comedy set in the 1970s. It follows a group of teenagers on the last day of school, with marijuana playing a central role in their escapades.

Stoner Animated Comedies

Some animated shows and films incorporate stoner humor and themes. These animations often take advantage of the medium to create visually and conceptually surreal situations.

Examples:

South Park is a well-known animated series that occasionally features stoner humor and cannabis-related storylines. The Harold & Kumar film series also incorporates animation sequences to enhance the comedy.

Elements of Stoner Comedy

What makes stoner comedies tick? Here are some key ingredients:

  • Marijuana Use: It’s the star of the show, leading to humorous situations and altered perceptions.
  • Countercultural Themes: These comedies often celebrate rebellion and non-conformity, flipping societal norms on their heads.
  • Humorous Paranoia: Characters can get hilariously paranoid, leading to comical situations that will leave you in stitches.
  • Slapstick Comedy: Expect plenty of physical comedy and slapstick humor to keep you laughing.
  • Absurdity: Stoner comedies thrive on absurd and surreal elements that add to the humor.
  • Laid-Back Characters: The main characters are typically relaxed, carefree, and sometimes a bit on the lazy side.

Features of Stoner Comedy

What sets stoner comedies apart? Here are some defining features:

  • Unconventional Humor: Stoner comedies go against the grain with their offbeat and unconventional brand of humor, catering to a specific audience.
  • Cultural Commentary: Some of these films use humor to take a dig at social and cultural issues, often through a satirical lens.
  • Musical Elements: Music plays a significant role in setting the mood and enhancing the humor of these films.
  • Cannabis Culture References: You’ll find plenty of nods to cannabis culture, including paraphernalia and slang.

More Examples

Now, let’s check out some stoner comedy classics:

  • Pineapple Express (2008) – A buddy stoner comedy starring Seth Rogen and James Franco as two unlikely friends on the run from drug dealers.
  • The Big Lebowski (1998) – While not exclusively a stoner comedy, it features The Dude Lebowski, a laid-back character who enjoys bowling and marijuana.
  • Super Troopers (2001) – This comedy follows a group of misfit highway patrol officers who engage in quirky and often stoned antics.
  • Harold & Kumar Go to White Castle (2004) – A buddy stoner comedy that follows the adventures of two friends on a quest to satisfy their craving for White Castle burgers.

FAQs about Stoner Comedy

What’s the main theme of stoner comedies?

Stoner comedies primarily revolve around the use of marijuana and the humorous situations it leads to.

Are stoner comedies just about getting high?

Not entirely! While marijuana use is a central theme, these films often explore countercultural themes, absurdity, and unique humor.

Who are some famous stoner comedy duos?

Cheech and Chong are legendary in the world of stoner comedy, known for their buddy stoner comedies.

Can stoner comedies be thought-provoking too?

Absolutely! Some stoner comedies use humor to comment on social and cultural issues in a satirical way.
