Is wild lettuce the same as milk thistle?

Identification can be a bit tricky because it closely resembles dandelion (Taraxacum) and milk thistle (Lactuca serriola). When young, wild lettuce has short leaves that grow in clusters. When you cut the stalk or leaves, you’ll see a white milky substance known as latex come out immediately.

How do you eliminate wild lettuce?

Chemical control for wild lettuce should be applied during the fall or spring. Herbicides should contain glyphosate, glufosinate or paraquat. Of the organic herbicide options, those that contain clove oil (eugenol) give the best results for wild lettuce control.

Can you eat Lactuca Serriola?
