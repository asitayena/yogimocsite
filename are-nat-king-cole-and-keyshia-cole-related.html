Nat King Cole: Career

A Musical Prodigy’s Journey

Nathaniel Adams Coles, renowned as Nat King Cole, graced the world with his presence on March 17, 1919, in the heart of Montgomery, Alabama.3

His parents, Edward and Perlina Coles, welcomed him into a household with three brothers and a half-sister.

Pianist in the Making

From the tender age of four, Nat King Cole embarked on his musical journey. His mother took on the role of his first piano instructor, and under her guidance, he swiftly transitioned into the hands of a church choir director.

By the age of 12, he delved into formal music lessons, immersing himself in the intricacies of jazz, western classical music, and gospel music.

The Chicago Connection

Growing up in Chicago, the vibrant epicenter of jazz, Cole’s enthusiasm for the genre flourished.

Even in his early teens, he would clandestinely slip out of his home to revel in the performances of renowned artists at various clubs across the city.
