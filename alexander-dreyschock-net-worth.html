FAQs about Alexander Dreyschock

Who was Alexander Dreyschock?

Alexander Dreyschock was a Czech pianist and composer, renowned for his exceptional left-hand technique and often compared to Liszt for his technical prowess.

What is Dreyschock famous for?

He is famous for playing the left-hand arpeggios of Chopin’s Revolutionary Étude in octaves at every concert, showcasing his extraordinary technical skill.

Did he have any notable students?

Yes, among his students was Arkady Abaza. Dreyschock held prestigious positions, including Court Pianist to the Tsar and Director of the Imperial School of Music for the Operatic Stage.

How did Alexander Dreyschock contribute to classical music?

Dreyschock’s contributions include his brilliant octaves, double sixths and thirds, and solos played by his left hand alone, which showcased his unparalleled technical abilities.

What were some notable works of Alexander Dreyschock?

His notable works include 3 Andantes et 4 Impromptus caractéristiques Op.3, Souvenir Op.4 Le Tremolo Etude, and Variations pour la main gauche seule Op.22 among others.
